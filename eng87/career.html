Mechanical Engineering Corporations and Career Paths

Automotive Engineering

Automotive engineers are a subset of mechanical engineers. They are tasked with designing, developing, and maintaining the physical structures of vehicles. When developing modern cars, automotive engineers must work closely with electrical and materials engineers.

Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning Engineering (HVAC)

Whenever any building has some form of heating or cooling, a mechanical engineer was likely involved in the design. Almost every habitable structure employs some form of heating, cooling, or ventilation. As a result, the HVAC industry maintains a steady demand for skilled mechanical engineers.

Robotics Engineering

Mechanical engineers are responsible for the development and maintenence of new and existing robotics systems. In addition to pure mechanical engineering skills, robotics engineers are often expected to have a strong background in writing, testing, and debugging code.

Solar energy

Mechanical engineering is crucial to the development of solar-powered infrastructure. Many solar arrays use moving parts that must be developed by mechanical engineers. In addition, the production of solar cells is automated. These autonomous systems must be designed and built by mechanical engineers. The rapid rise of solar energy in recent years means that solar-based engineering jobs are in high demand.
